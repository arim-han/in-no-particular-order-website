‘In No Particular Order’ is an archive of German and Korean proverbs and figures of speech. Collected and designed by Nicole, who is from Germany, and Arim, who is Korean-American, this project is a reflection of the conversations we have shared about each other’s spoken languages.

Learning proverbs from another culture is often accompanied by moments of confusion. It is not uncommon to wonder what a monkey, a sausage, or a cookie has to do with the meaning of an expression. Yet these moments of uncertainty are an important part of cultural exchange. Rather than removing them, we wanted to translate them into the design of the archive itself.

Nicole selected Korean proverbs, while Arim selected German ones. Each proverb was translated into English and accompanied by our own interpretation of its meaning. In exploring the archive, we encourage you to discover both cultures through chance encounters and personal interpretation. Nothing is arranged in a particular order.

Caution: May contain accidental wisdom.

Have fun exploring, Arim & Nicole
